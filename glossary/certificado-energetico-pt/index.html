Portugal Legal Term

Certificado Energético

Portuguese: Certificado Energético (Energy Certificate)

Mandatory Portuguese energy performance certificate (EPC) rating a property from A+ to F. Required for all property sales and rentals. Must be issued by a certified expert registered with ADENE.

TL;DRMandatory Portuguese energy performance certificate (EPC) rating a property from A+ to F. Required for all property sales and rentals. Must be issued by a certified expert registered with ADENE.

The certificado energético (energy performance certificate) is a mandatory document for all Portuguese property sales and rentals, rating the property's energy efficiency from A+ (most efficient) to F (least efficient). It is issued by a certified energy expert (perito qualificado) registered with ADENE (Agência para a Energia), the national energy agency that administers the Portuguese energy certification system (SCE — Sistema de Certificação Energética).

The certificate is valid for 10 years and must be provided to the buyer or tenant before the signing of the sale or rental agreement. In the case of sales, the reference number of the certificado energético must appear in the promissory contract (CPCV) and the final escritura. Failure to obtain and disclose the certificate can expose the seller to fines and legal liability to the buyer.

The energy rating affects the value of Portuguese property — particularly as EU energy efficiency regulations tighten. Properties rated F or G (the old scale) face restrictions on long-term rentals across the EU from 2030 under proposed EPBD (Energy Performance of Buildings Directive) changes. While exact Portuguese implementation is still evolving, buyers of low-rated properties should budget for energy renovation costs.

For older Portuguese buildings — particularly stone-built rural properties and pre-1960 Lisbon and Porto apartments — achieving a good energy rating is genuinely difficult. Single-glazed windows, solid stone walls, and oil or gas heating systems are common in desirable historical properties, all contributing to poor ratings. An F or G rated property is not unsaleable, but may require significant investment in insulation, windows, and heating systems to comply with future rental regulations.

The cost of obtaining a Portuguese certificado energético varies by property size and location, typically ranging from €150 to €400 for residential properties. The perito qualificado inspects the property, assesses the construction type, heating and cooling systems, hot water provision, and natural lighting, and produces the certificate through the ADENE online system.

⚠ Common MistakeBuying a Portuguese property for rental investment without checking the energy rating. F-rated properties face increasing regulatory risk under EU energy efficiency rules, potentially becoming unlettable without renovation. The renovation cost to improve an F-rated stone house to C could easily exceed €30,000.
💡 Expert TipAsk the seller for the certificado energético reference number at the outset of negotiations, not at the CPCV stage. You can then look up the certificate on ADENE's portal (SCE.pt) to see the full report, including the specific measures recommended to improve the rating — useful information for renovation budgeting before you commit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays for the energy certificate in a Portuguese property sale?

The seller pays for the certificado energético — it is the seller's obligation to provide it. It cannot be passed on to the buyer as an additional cost. If a seller asks you to pay for their energy certificate, decline.

Does the energy certificate apply to rural properties and agricultural land?

Agricultural buildings, standalone garages, and properties not intended for human habitation are generally exempt from the energy certification requirement. However, farm houses and quintas used for residential purposes are covered. Historic buildings under specific monument protection may also be exempt.

Can I use a certificate obtained for a previous sale?

Yes — if the certificate is still within its 10-year validity period and the property has not undergone significant alterations since it was issued, the existing certificate may be reused. If major works have been done (new heating system, insulation, windows), a new certificate should be obtained to reflect the improvement.
