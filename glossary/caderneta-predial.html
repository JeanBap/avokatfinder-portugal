Caderneta Predial (Land Registry Extract)

An official certificate from the Portuguese land registry proving ownership, property boundaries, and encumbrances on real property.

Definition

The Caderneta Predial is an official certificate issued by the Portuguese land registry (Conservatória do Registo Predial) that serves as the primary proof of property ownership and legal status. This document contains essential information including the name of the registered owner, the property's fiscal identification number (article number), detailed description of the property boundaries and area, and any encumbrances such as mortgages, liens, or easements affecting the property. The caderneta predial is required for nearly all property transactions in Portugal, including sales, purchases, mortgages, inheritances, and the establishment of usufruct rights. The accuracy of the information in the caderneta predial is crucial, as it forms the legal basis for property rights and is relied upon by banks, courts, and governmental agencies. Any discrepancies between the physical property and the registry must be corrected through formal procedures to avoid legal complications.

Obtaining a caderneta predial is straightforward and can be done by the property owner or an authorized representative at the local conservatória do registo predial (land registry office). The document is issued upon request and payment of a small fee, typically processed within a few days. The certificate includes current information as of the date of issuance, showing all registered rights, mortgages, and claims against the property. Property owners in Portugal should keep a current copy of their caderneta predial on file, as it is regularly requested for various legal and financial purposes. The document is not confidential and can be requested by potential buyers, tenants, lenders, or other parties with legitimate interest in the property.

The caderneta predial lists all mortgages and encumbrances registered against the property, which is essential information for any potential buyer or lender. If a property is mortgaged, this appears clearly in the caderneta predial, and the property cannot be freely transferred without the lender's consent or payoff of the mortgage. Easements (rights of way, utility easements, or other rights benefiting neighboring properties) also appear on the caderneta predial and continue to bind the property even after sale. Understanding all encumbrances is critical before purchasing property, as the buyer will be bound by them. Legal counsel should review the caderneta predial to explain any restrictions or charges before a purchase is finalized.

If information in the caderneta predial is incorrect or outdated, corrections can be made through formal procedures at the conservatória do registo predial. Common issues include incorrect property boundaries, omitted or incorrectly described property improvements, or outdated owner information. Correction procedures vary depending on whether the error is administrative or involves substantive rights disputes. Administrative corrections can often be made by agreement between parties and the registry office, while disputes over property boundaries or ownership may require court proceedings. Given the importance of accurate registry information, any discrepancies should be addressed promptly with professional legal assistance.

In modern property transactions, the caderneta predial has been supplemented by electronic property registry systems, but it remains the official legal document proving ownership and encumbrances. Electronic copies have the same legal force as printed certificates. Property buyers should always request and carefully review the caderneta predial before making any purchase, as it reveals the true legal status of the property. Banks and insurance companies require current copies before providing mortgages or property insurance. The caderneta predial is your definitive source for confirming you have clear title to property or understanding what restrictions and obligations attach to your ownership.

Key Facts

  • Official certificate from Portuguese land registry proving ownership and legal status of property
  • Contains property description, owner information, and all encumbrances and mortgages
  • Required for all property sales, purchases, mortgages, and inheritances
  • Can be obtained by owner or authorized representative at local conservatória do registo predial
  • Reveals all legal restrictions and rights affecting the property

Common Mistake

Assuming a property is free of mortgages without checking the caderneta predial; always verify the certificate before purchase.

Expert Tip

Request an updated caderneta predial just before finalizing a property purchase to confirm no new encumbrances have been registered since your initial review.

Frequently Asked Questions

How current is the information in a caderneta predial?

The caderneta predial shows the status of property as of the date it is issued. Since new mortgages or liens can be registered at any time, it is advisable to request an updated certificate shortly before closing a property purchase.

Can anyone request a caderneta predial or only the owner?

Anyone can request a caderneta predial from the land registry office. The document is a public record, and potential buyers, lenders, and other parties with legitimate interest in the property can obtain copies by paying the required fee.

What should I do if the caderneta predial shows an error in the property description?

Contact the local conservatória do registo predial to file a correction request. Simple administrative errors can often be corrected quickly, while boundary disputes may require surveying and court approval. Hire a lawyer to guide you through the correction process.
