Portugal Legal Term

Mais-Valias

Portuguese: Mais-Valias (Capital Gains)

Portuguese capital gains tax on property sales. For residents, gains are aggregated with income and taxed at progressive rates up to 48%. For non-residents, a flat 28% rate applies (EU residents may elect progressive rates).

TL;DRPortuguese capital gains tax on property sales. For residents, gains are aggregated with income and taxed at progressive rates up to 48%. For non-residents, a flat 28% rate applies (EU residents may elect progressive rates).

Mais-valias (capital gains) from Portuguese property sales are taxable in Portugal regardless of where the seller lives, under the principle that gains from immovable property are taxable in the country where the property is located. The calculation is: sale price minus acquisition cost (including purchase taxes, notary fees, estate agent commission, and documented improvement works) minus an inflation adjustment (coeficiente de desvalorização da moeda) applied to the acquisition cost.

For Portuguese tax residents, 50% of the net gain is included in taxable income and taxed at progressive IRS (income tax) rates, which reach 48% (plus solidarity surcharge for higher earners). This means the effective CGT rate for residents ranges from roughly 14% to 24% of the full gain depending on total income. The 50% inclusion rule (exclusão de 50%) makes Portuguese CGT more favourable than a flat rate for lower-income sellers.

For non-residents, a flat 28% rate applies to 100% of the gain — significantly higher than the effective rate for most resident sellers. However, since a 2023 ruling, EU/EEA non-resident sellers may elect to be taxed as residents (applying the 50% inclusion at progressive rates) if this produces a lower liability. UK nationals post-Brexit cannot elect this treatment and face the 28% flat rate on the full gain.

The primary residence exemption (exclusão da habitação própria permanente) is Portugal's most important CGT relief: if the gain from selling your primary residence is reinvested in another primary residence within 36 months of the sale (or 24 months before), the gain is fully exempt. This reinvestment can be in another Portuguese property or in an EU/EEA property. Since 2023, the reinvestment can also be used to pay down a mortgage on your existing primary residence.

Non-habitual resident (NHR) status holders in Portugal were historically exempt from foreign-source capital gains. For Portuguese-source property gains, NHR offered no exemption — the standard rules applied. The NHR regime was substantially reformed from 2024 (replaced by IFICI for new applicants), but existing NHR holders retain their status until expiry.

⚠ Common MistakeFailing to reinvest the gain from selling your Portuguese primary residence within 36 months to claim the exemption. The clock starts on the sale date — even if you are abroad or buying elsewhere, the 36-month window runs. Missing it means paying full CGT that could have been entirely avoided.
💡 Expert TipKeep all purchase receipts, improvement invoices, and real estate agent invoices from the day you buy any Portuguese property. These documented costs directly reduce your mais-valias base when you sell — losing them inflates your taxable gain and your tax bill.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Portugal tax capital gains on Portuguese property if I am not tax resident?

Yes — Portugal taxes gains on Portuguese property regardless of where the seller lives, as the source-country taxation principle applies. You must file a Portuguese IRS non-resident return in the year of sale to declare the gain, even if you have never lived in Portugal.

Can I deduct the cost of renovations from my mais-valias?

Yes — documented improvement works (obras de conservação e valorização) carried out in the 12 years preceding the sale are deductible. You need receipted invoices from registered contractors. DIY works, cash payments, and work by unregistered tradespeople cannot be deducted.

How does the reinvestment exemption work if I buy my new home before selling the old one?

The reinvestment window works in both directions: you may buy the new primary residence up to 24 months before selling the old one, or up to 36 months after. The key is that both properties must qualify as your primary residence (habitação própria permanente) and you must declare the reinvestment intention in your IRS return for the year of sale.
