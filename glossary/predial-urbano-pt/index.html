Predial Urbano and IMI (Urban Property and Annual Property Tax)

IMI (Imposto Municipal sobre Imoveis) is Portugal's annual property tax on urban and rural properties, assessed by the Financas on the property's valor patrimonial tributario (VPT). Rates vary by municipality.

IMI (Imposto Municipal sobre Imoveis) is Portugal's annual municipal property tax, equivalent to the UK's council tax or France's taxe fonciere but calculated as a percentage of the property's official tax value (VPT — valor patrimonial tributario). It is levied on every owner of Portuguese real estate as of 31 December of the tax year.

Rates for urban property (predios urbanos — buildings and their land) range from 0.3% to 0.45% of the VPT depending on the municipality. Lisbon currently applies 0.3%; other municipalities set their own rate within the statutory band. Rural property (predios rusticos) is taxed at 0.8%. Property held by offshore entities in blacklisted jurisdictions is taxed at 7.5%.

IMI is assessed automatically by Financas and sent as an annual notice in April/May, payable by 30 April (if under EUR 500), by two instalments in April and November (EUR 500–1,000), or three instalments in April, July, and November (over EUR 1,000).

The VPT is updated over time through general revaluations (avaliacoes gerais) or when a property is sold (triggering a new assessment). The VPT of older properties in prime locations is often well below market value — meaning IMI bills are lower than buyers expect. Conversely, new builds are assessed at construction cost, often producing higher VPTs relative to market value.

IMI exemptions exist for primary residences below a VPT threshold (currently around EUR 125,000) and for families with dependent children (additional reductions). Buyers should register their property as habitacao propria e permanente with Financas to access these exemptions.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not registering the property as your primary residence (habitacao propria e permanente) with Financas means you miss the IMI exemption and pay a higher rate. Do this registration promptly after the escritura.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask the seller for their most recent IMI notice before exchange — it shows the current VPT and annual tax liability. A low VPT on an older property in a prime area can be a significant ongoing advantage for the buyer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the VPT and how is it calculated?

The valor patrimonial tributario is the official tax value calculated by Financas using a formula based on construction cost, location, quality, age, and usable area. It is not the same as market value and is often lower.

Can I challenge my property's VPT?

Yes. If you believe the VPT is incorrect (wrong area, wrong quality rating), you can request a secondary evaluation (2a avaliacao) from Financas within 30 days of notification. A panel of independent experts reassesses the property.

Does the new owner start paying IMI immediately?

IMI is assessed on the owner as of 31 December. The buyer pays for any year in which they own the property on 31 December. Prorations between buyer and seller are common in contracts but are private arrangements — Financas bills whoever is the registered owner.
