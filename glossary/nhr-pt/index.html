NHR (Non-Habitual Resident Tax Regime)

Portugal's Non-Habitual Resident (NHR) regime offered foreign income tax exemptions and a 20% flat rate on certain Portuguese income for 10 years. A reformed NHR 2.0 programme replaced it from 2024 with narrower eligibility.

Portugal's original NHR regime, introduced in 2009, attracted hundreds of thousands of foreign residents by offering a 10-year period of preferential tax treatment: foreign-source income (pensions, dividends, rental income, capital gains from abroad) was often exempt from Portuguese income tax, and qualifying Portuguese-source income was taxed at a flat 20% rather than the standard progressive rates up to 48%.

The original NHR closed to new applicants at the end of 2023 as part of political changes responding to housing affordability concerns. Anyone who became a Portuguese tax resident before 31 December 2023 and applied within the statutory deadline retains their 10-year NHR status.

From 2024, a replacement scheme called IFICI (Incentivo Fiscal a Investigacao Cientifica e Inovacao — Tax Incentive for Scientific Research and Innovation) — informally known as NHR 2.0 — took effect. It is significantly narrower: it applies only to qualifying professions including researchers, technology and innovation professionals, and highly qualified roles in specific sectors. Retirees and passive income investors are no longer eligible.

For property buyers considering Portugal primarily for the NHR tax benefit, the 2024 reform is a fundamental change. Those who qualified under the original scheme retain their status, but the broad passive-income exemptions are no longer available to new arrivals.

The interaction of NHR (or IFICI) with the buyer's home-country tax obligations is complex. Double taxation treaties and domestic laws vary significantly. Specialist Portuguese and home-country tax advice is essential before relocating on the assumption of tax savings.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming the original NHR tax benefits still apply to new arrivals in Portugal after 2023 is incorrect. The IFICI scheme has different eligibility rules. Verify your specific situation with a qualified Portuguese tax lawyer before making any relocation decision based on tax.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If you are still eligible for the original NHR (you became tax resident before end-2023), confirm you formally applied for NHR status within the required timeframe. Late applications may still be accepted in some circumstances — consult a tax lawyer urgently if you missed the deadline.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I still get NHR benefits if I move to Portugal now?

Only if you qualify under the new IFICI scheme (NHR 2.0), which is restricted to specific professional categories. Broad passive income tax exemptions available under the original NHR are not available to new applicants from 2024.

Does the NHR regime exempt all foreign income?

Under the original NHR, many categories of foreign income were exempt, but the rules were complex and dependent on treaty provisions. Under IFICI, the benefits are structured differently. Never assume exemption without specific legal advice.

Does the NHR status affect my property purchase in Portugal?

NHR/IFICI is a personal income tax regime and does not directly affect IMT (transfer tax) or IMI (annual property tax). It affects how your income — including future rental income and capital gains from the Portuguese property — is taxed.
