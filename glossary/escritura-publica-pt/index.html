Escritura Publica (Public Deed / Notarial Deed)

The escritura publica is Portugal's notarial deed of sale — the formal document executed before a public notary that legally transfers property ownership. No property transfer is valid without it.

The escritura publica (public deed) is the centrepiece of every Portuguese property transaction. It is executed before a licensed notary (notario) or, since 2008, can alternatively be processed at a lawyer's office via the 'Casa Pronta' (Ready Home) one-stop shop service, which combines the deed, registration, and tax payment in a single appointment.

Before the escritura is signed, the parties must present: the promissory purchase and sale agreement (Contrato Promessa de Compra e Venda — CPCV) if one was signed; proof of IMT payment; proof of Imposto do Selo payment; the property's energy performance certificate (certificado energetico); and, for new builds, the licence of use (licenca de utilizacao). The notary checks all documents and reads the deed aloud before the parties sign.

The escritura must include exact property identification (article number from the land registry — conservatoria do registo predial — and the matricula number from the Financas records). Any discrepancy between these records must be resolved before the deed can be executed.

Following execution, the notary submits the deed for registration at the Conservatoria do Registo Predial (land registry). Registration is what makes the ownership change enforceable against third parties. Until registration, the buyer has a personal right against the seller but not an absolute property right.

The Casa Pronta service, available at notary offices and at some branches of Citizen Shops (Lojas do Cidadao), charges a fixed fee that covers notary fees, land registry registration, and tax filing in one appointment — typically within one to two hours.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Arriving at the escritura without confirming that the IMT and Imposto do Selo payments have cleared at the bank is a common delay. The notary will not proceed without proof of payment — confirm clearance the morning before the appointment.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Use the Casa Pronta service if your transaction is straightforward. It is faster, cheaper, and more convenient than separate notary and land registry appointments. Book via the dedicated Casa Pronta portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Contrato Promessa de Compra e Venda (CPCV)?

A binding promissory contract signed before the escritura, typically accompanied by a deposit of 10–30%. If the buyer withdraws, the deposit is forfeited. If the seller withdraws, they must return double the deposit.

Can I sign a Portuguese escritura remotely?

Yes, via notarial power of attorney (procuracao) granted to a Portuguese lawyer. The POA must be apostilled if executed abroad. This is the standard route for foreign buyers who cannot attend in person.

How long after signing does registration take?

Registration is typically completed within a few weeks of submission. The land registry issues a certificate (certidao) confirming the new owner. Banks will not release mortgage funds until the mortgage charge is also registered.
