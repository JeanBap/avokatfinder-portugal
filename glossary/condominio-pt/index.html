Portugal Legal Term

Condomínio

Portuguese: Condomínio (Condominium)

Portuguese apartment building co-ownership structure where owners share common areas and costs through a mandatory owners' association. Each owner pays a condominium fee (quota condominial) and votes at annual assemblies.

TL;DRPortuguese apartment building co-ownership structure where owners share common areas and costs through a mandatory owners' association. Each owner pays a condominium fee (quota condominial) and votes at annual assemblies.

A condomínio in Portugal is the legal regime governing multi-owner apartment buildings and developments, where individual units (fracções autónomas) are privately owned and common areas (partes comuns) — including the building structure, roof, lifts, garden, and shared facilities — are jointly owned by all unit owners in proportion to their permilagem (thousandths share).

Every condomínio must have an administrador (administrator), who may be one of the owners or a professional property management company (empresa de gestão condominial). The administrador manages day-to-day operations, calls the annual assembleia de condóminos (owners' meeting), administers the building's accounts, and enforces the regulamento do condomínio (building rules). Portuguese law requires condomínios to maintain a fundo de reserva (reserve fund) of at least 10% of annual common costs.

The quota condominial (monthly condominium fee) covers: building insurance, cleaning, communal lighting, lift maintenance, garden upkeep, administrator fees, and the reserve fund contribution. In Lisbon and Porto, monthly condominium fees for well-managed buildings typically range from €50 to €300+ per month for standard apartments, with luxury developments charging significantly more.

Before purchasing a Portuguese apartment, request from the administrador: the last three years of assembleia minutes (actas), the current annual budget, the fundo de reserva balance, and confirmation that all outstanding quotas are paid. Portuguese law provides that unpaid condominium fees (dívidas de condomínio) follow the property — not the owner — meaning you as buyer can inherit the previous owner's arrears if you do not obtain a clean bill.

Decisions at the assembleia de condóminos require different voting thresholds. Routine administration requires simple majority by permilagem. Major works require two-thirds majority. Changes to the título constitutivo (the founding document defining units and common areas) require unanimity. As an expat owner who cannot attend meetings, grant a proxy (procuração) to a trusted representative or your property manager.

⚠ Common MistakeNot requesting a written declaration from the administrador confirming zero outstanding dívidas de condomínio before signing the CPCV. Portuguese condominium debts attach to the property, not the person — you could purchase an apartment and immediately owe years of unpaid fees accumulated by the previous owner.
💡 Expert TipAsk the administrador for the actas (minutes) of the last three annual assemblies before making an offer. The minutes reveal any planned major works voted but not yet funded, ongoing disputes between owners, and the real financial health of the building — information not visible from a property viewing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I rent my Portuguese apartment on Airbnb without the condomínio's approval?

Portuguese law allows short-term rental (alojamento local) in privately owned apartments, but the condomínio can vote to restrict or prohibit tourist accommodation in the building by a two-thirds majority vote. Many Lisbon and Porto buildings have done exactly this. Check the regulamento and recent actas before purchasing for short-term rental.

What happens if I stop paying my quota condominial?

The administrador can take you to court for unpaid quotas. A court judgment creates an executory title allowing enforcement against your assets — including the apartment itself. Portuguese courts regularly grant enforcement orders for condominium debts, and the process is faster than general civil litigation.

How do I find out who the administrador is for a building I want to buy in?

The administrador's identity is typically registered with the Conservatória do Registo Predial (land registry) or Finanças (tax authority). Your real estate agent or notário can identify them. Alternatively, the building entrance usually displays the administrador's contact details by law.
