Condominio (Condominium / Owners Community)

A condominio is the Portuguese owners community for a multi-unit building, responsible for managing common areas, maintenance, and collective costs. Every apartment owner is a mandatory member and co-owner of the common parts.

In Portugal, when a building contains two or more autonomous units (fracoes autonomas) — whether residential or commercial — the owners form a condominio governed by the Civil Code and the Horizontal Property Regime (Regime da Propriedade Horizontal). Each unit owner holds full freehold title to their specific fraction and an undivided proportional share of the common parts (partes comuns).

The condominio is governed by two bodies: the assembleia de condominios (general assembly of all owners) which meets at least annually; and the administrador do condominio (building manager/administrator) elected by the assembly. Large condominios typically appoint a professional management company as administrador.

Each owner pays a monthly or quarterly quota de condominio (maintenance contribution), proportional to their unit's permilagem (per-thousand share of the building). The quota covers building insurance, communal utilities, cleaning, lift maintenance, and contributions to the fundo de reserva (reserve fund), which is legally required to be at least 10% of the annual budget.

Before purchasing a Portuguese apartment, always request: the condominio's last two years of accounts (contas do condominio), the current budget, confirmation of any outstanding works planned, and a declaration that the selling unit has no outstanding quota debts. Unpaid quotas create a charge on the property — the buyer may inherit them.

Disputes between owners, or between an owner and the administrador, can be submitted to a Tribunal Arbitral de Condominio (condominium arbitration tribunal) — a specialist body that resolves condominio disputes more quickly than the courts.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Skipping the check for outstanding condominio debt on the unit you are buying is a costly oversight. In Portugal, condominio debts follow the property — the new owner can be pursued for the previous owner's arrears.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Request a declaracao de divida (debt declaration) from the condominio administrator specifically stating that the unit has no outstanding quota debts. This document should be obtained just before the escritura and presented to the notary.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the permilagem?

The per-thousand share that represents each unit's proportional ownership of the common parts. It determines the owner's voting weight in the assembly and their contribution to shared costs.

Can the condominio prevent me from renovating my apartment?

Internal works that do not affect the structure, facade, or common services can generally proceed without condominio approval. Structural changes, facade alterations, or works affecting shared systems require assembly approval.

What is the fundo de reserva?

The statutory reserve fund that condominios must maintain, at a minimum of 10% of the annual budget. It is intended to fund major maintenance and emergency repairs. A depleted reserve fund is a warning sign.
