Portugal Legal Term

Arrendamento

Portuguese: Arrendamento (Tenancy / Lease)

Portuguese tenancy agreement governed by the Regime do Arrendamento Urbano (RAU/NRAU). Residential tenancies have statutory protections including notice periods and rent update rules. Key distinction: long-term vs. short-term (alojamento local).

TL;DRPortuguese tenancy agreement governed by the Regime do Arrendamento Urbano (RAU/NRAU). Residential tenancies have statutory protections including notice periods and rent update rules. Key distinction: long-term vs. short-term (alojamento local).

Arrendamento is the Portuguese term for a tenancy or lease agreement. Residential tenancies in Portugal are governed by the Regime do Arrendamento Urbano (RAU) as updated by the Novo Regime do Arrendamento Urbano (NRAU, Law 6/2006 and subsequent amendments). The NRAU provides substantial tenant protections while attempting to balance landlord rights — a balance that has shifted multiple times in recent years as Portugal has faced housing affordability crises.

Standard residential tenancy contracts (contratos de arrendamento para habitação) must be in writing, registered with the Finanças (tax authority) within 30 days, and are subject to minimum notice periods: landlords must give a minimum of 2 months' notice for contracts under 1 year, up to 4 years' notice for contracts over 6 years. Tenants can terminate with 2 months' notice at any time after the initial term.

Rent updates (actualizações de renda) for existing residential tenancies are limited to annual increases based on the official coefficient published by the INE (Instituto Nacional de Estatística). Since 2023, Portugal has capped annual rent increases for existing tenancies at 2% (the "extraordinary regime" introduced amid the housing crisis) — significantly below CPI in recent years, meaning real rental yields have declined for many landlords.

A critical distinction for investor-owners: the alojamento local (AL — short-term tourist accommodation) regime operates entirely outside the NRAU. Properties let on platforms like Airbnb for periods under 30 days are subject to their own licensing regime (registo de alojamento local), tax rules (Category B income), and municipal controls. Some Lisbon and Porto parishes have banned new AL licences entirely.

For expat landlords renting out Portuguese property: rental income is taxable in Portugal at 28% for non-resident landlords, or at progressive IRS rates if resident. Expenses including mortgage interest, condominium fees, maintenance, and management fees are deductible under the simplified regime (categoria F) or the more complex régime contabilidade organizada. Annual registration of the rental contract with the Finanças and issuance of rent receipts (recibos de renda) via the AT portal are mandatory.

Lisbon and Porto have closed many parishes to new AL registrations — and purchasing an investment property in a restricted area significantly limits your rental strategy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I terminate a Portuguese tenancy to sell the property?

Yes — but with significant notice requirements. For contracts over 6 years, you must give 4 years' notice. Alternatively, you may sell the property with the sitting tenant (the buyer inherits the tenancy) or wait for natural contract expiry. Portuguese law also gives sitting tenants a right of first refusal (preferência) when the property is sold.

What is the tenant's right of preferência (first refusal)?

Under Portuguese law, a landlord selling a rented property must offer the sitting tenant the right to purchase at the same price and conditions agreed with the buyer. The tenant has 8 working days to exercise this right. Failure to offer preferência gives the tenant the right to void the sale within 6 months.

Are there any tax incentives for long-term rentals in Portugal?

Yes — landlords who let at affordable rents under the Programa de Arrendamento Acessível (affordable rental programme) benefit from a reduced 5% tax rate on rental income. Long-term rentals (2+ years) also benefit from a 5% tax rate on the portion of income above a new threshold introduced in 2023 as part of the Mais Habitação programme. Tax rules in this area have changed frequently — verify current rates with a Portuguese tax adviser.
