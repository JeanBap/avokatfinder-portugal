IMT (Municipal Property Transfer Tax)

IMT (Imposto Municipal sobre as Transmissoes Onerosas de Imoveis) is Portugal's main property purchase tax, levied on the buyer at progressive rates up to 8% depending on the property value and intended use.

IMT is the primary tax cost of buying Portuguese property. It is calculated on the higher of the declared purchase price and the tax-assessed value (valor patrimonial tributario — VPT) held in the Financas (tax authority) records. The applicable rate depends on the property type, location (mainland vs. Azores/Madeira), and intended use (primary residence, secondary residence, or commercial).

For mainland Portugal residential property used as a primary habitual residence (habitacao propria e permanente), IMT rates are progressive: 0% up to approximately EUR 92,000; 2% from EUR 92,000–126,000; 5% from EUR 126,000–172,000; 7% from EUR 172,000–287,000; 8% from EUR 287,000–574,000; and a flat rate for properties above EUR 574,000 (subject to regular indexing). First-time buyers under 35 purchasing primary residences were exempt from IMT up to EUR 316,000 under a 2023 measure — verify the current thresholds with a lawyer.

Secondary residences and investment properties are charged at a flat 6% IMT rate. Rustic land (predio rustico) attracts 5%. Commercial property is at 6.5%.

IMT is collected by the notary on the day of the escritura publica (public deed). It must be paid before the deed is signed — the Financas generates a payment slip (guia de pagamento) that must be settled at a bank, post office, or online, and the proof of payment is presented at the notary's office.

Non-resident buyers pay the same IMT rates as residents. Properties purchased through offshore vehicles attract an 8% flat rate with no progression.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Using a transfer price below the VPT to reduce IMT is not effective — the tax is calculated on whichever figure is higher. And declaring a price below actual value is tax fraud with criminal consequences.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Calculate your IMT before making an offer, not after. Use the Financas IMT simulator (available online) to estimate the charge based on the property's VPT and intended use. It takes two minutes and could save significant budget surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the VPT?

The valor patrimonial tributario is the official tax-assessed value of a property, held in the Financas records. It is used as the IMT floor and for annual IMI (municipal property tax) calculations.

Is IMT the same as stamp duty?

No. Portugal also charges Imposto do Selo (stamp duty) at 0.8% of the purchase price on property transfers, separate from IMT. Both are paid on the day of the escritura.

Do I pay IMT on a new build from a developer?

Typically yes, unless the developer is selling in the course of their business and VAT applies instead. New build transactions from VAT-registered developers are usually IMT-exempt but subject to VAT on the construction component.
