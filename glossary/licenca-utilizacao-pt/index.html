Licenca de Utilizacao (Licence of Use / Habitation Permit)

The licenca de utilizacao (formerly licenca de habitabilidade) is the Portuguese habitation permit confirming that a building or unit complies with planning rules and is legally fit for its stated use. It is required for all property sales.

The licenca de utilizacao (use licence) is issued by the local municipality (camara municipal) and confirms that a building or unit has been constructed in accordance with its approved building permit and meets the legal requirements for its stated use — residential, commercial, or services. Without a valid licenca, a property cannot legally be sold or rented.

For properties built before August 1951, no licenca de utilizacao is required — such properties are assumed to have acquired their use status through long-established occupation, and their sale is lawful without one. For all properties built after this date, the licenca is mandatory and must be presented at the notary on the day of the escritura.

Illegal constructions (construcoes ilegais) — extensions, outbuildings, or converted spaces built without a building permit — do not have a licenca de utilizacao for those specific areas. This is extremely common in older Portuguese properties, particularly in rural areas and village settings. An illegal extension may render a property harder to mortgage and can create liability for the owner to restore the original permitted state.

For new builds, the licenca de utilizacao is issued after the municipal inspection (vistoria) confirms that the finished building matches its approved project. The developer cannot legally hand over the property or allow occupation until this licence is issued.

Buyers should request the caderneta predial (property tax record from Financas) and the certidao de teor (land registry certificate) alongside the licenca, as together these three documents confirm the property's legal identity, ownership, and permitted use.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Buying a property with illegal extensions without quantifying the regularisation cost or confirming they can be regularised is a common mistake. Some illegal works cannot be regularised at all if they violate planning rules that cannot be retrospectively waived.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For rural properties in particular, ask the seller for the licenca specifically for each structure on the plot — the main house, any outbuildings, pools, and annexes. Each structure needs its own permit history reviewed.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a construcao ilegal?

An illegal construction is any structure built without the required building permit or built differently from what was approved. Even small extensions or conversions can be illegal if they were not permitted.

Can I get a mortgage on a property with illegal extensions?

Most Portuguese banks will not lend against the value of unpermitted areas. Some will lend only against the permitted floor area, which may be significantly less than the advertised total area.

How do I regularise an illegal construction?

The process (legalizacao) involves applying to the camara municipal for retrospective approval. Success depends on whether the illegal structure complies with current planning rules. In areas of ecological or architectural sensitivity, approval may be impossible.
