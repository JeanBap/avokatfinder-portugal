CPCV (Contrato Promessa de Compra e Venda)

The CPCV is Portugal's binding preliminary purchase agreement, typically accompanied by a deposit of 10–30%. If the seller withdraws, they pay double the deposit; if the buyer withdraws, the deposit is forfeited.

The Contrato Promessa de Compra e Venda (CPCV) is the binding preliminary contract that commits both parties to complete a Portuguese property purchase. It is signed before the escritura publica (notarial deed) — often weeks or months earlier — once the parties have agreed price and key terms but are not yet ready for final completion.

The CPCV must be in writing but does not require a notary. However, if the property is under construction or the seller is a company, the CPCV should be notarially recognised (reconhecimento de assinaturas) to ensure enforceability. For most residential purchases, a lawyer-drafted CPCV signed by both parties is sufficient.

The standard deposit (sinal) is 10–30% of the agreed purchase price. Under Portuguese law, the sinal operates as a penalty: if the buyer withdraws (incumprimento do promitente-comprador), the seller keeps the full deposit. If the seller withdraws (incumprimento do promitente-vendedor), they must return double the deposit (sinal em dobro). This rule is statutory — it applies even if the contract does not explicitly state it.

The CPCV should include a completion date (data prevista para a escritura), conditions precedent (mortgage approval, survey results, licence checks), and provisions for what happens if completion is delayed. Without a completion date, enforcing the contract becomes complicated.

For new build purchases or purchases off-plan, the developer is required to have a bank guarantee (garantia bancaria) covering the buyer's deposit if the building is not yet built. This protects buyers in the event of developer insolvency.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Signing a CPCV without a clear completion date and without conditions precedent for financing approval leaves you contractually bound with no exit if your mortgage is refused. Always include a financing condition and a definite completion timeline.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Do not transfer the CPCV deposit directly to the seller — use a lawyer's client account as escrow. If the title is subsequently found to be flawed, recovering the deposit directly from a seller is far harder than from a regulated lawyer's account.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the CPCV be cancelled by mutual agreement?

Yes. Both parties can agree to rescind the CPCV and agree how the deposit is handled. Without mutual agreement, the statutory sinal rules apply and court action may be required to enforce withdrawal.

Does the CPCV need to be registered?

Registration with the Conservatoria do Registo Predial is not mandatory but is recommended for purchases of large value or long-duration contracts. Registration gives the buyer's interest priority against third-party claims.

What happens if the escritura is delayed beyond the CPCV date?

Either party can serve formal notice (interpelacao admonitoria) setting a final deadline after which they will treat the contract as breached. If breach is established, the sinal rules apply and the aggrieved party can seek specific performance through the courts.
